For the last several years I've been working with keyword-driven wiki-based UI test frameworks, most recently Selenesse , a mashup of Fitnesse and Selenium-RC (https://github.com/marisaseal/selenesse, which I help create), before that SWAT (http://ulti-swat.wikispaces.com , Fitnesse plus a proprietary driver for IE like old Watir but in C#), before that Wikitests (Socialtext wiki plus Selenium https://www.socialtext.net/open/wiki_tests).

These frameworks solve some problems but create others. The very high level of abstraction makes it possible for non-coding testers to create well-designed tests. The possibilities for ATDD/specification-by-example using the Fitnesse SLIM engine are remarkable. Such frameworks are great for in-house whole-team approaches to browser testing. I'll have a demo of Selenesse to share to demonstrate some of the goodness of this approach.

However, making such frameworks useful requires managing a lot of fiddly bits that are unsuitable for frameworks exposed to a greater community of outsiders. One reason I want to attend the Bazaar is to gather ideas about how to make code-only frameworks (e.g. Watirsplash or Taza) as user-friendly as possible. I anticipate that in the very near future I'll be moving away from wiki-based based frameworks and toward code-only frameworks, and I would like to share the knowledge I have while it is still fresh.
