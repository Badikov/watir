"Managing Test Automation - managing yourself and/or your team. "
Not sure this is really in line with what you have in mind for the conference focus, but wanted to volunteer some kind of presentation... I think others would do better than me on more Watir-specific and specification-by-example topics.
The below types of discussions have been popular and useful in my N. Virginia Test Automation Interest Group monthly meetings.
My preference would be to do this as a very discussion-oriented participatory presentation.
--======================
While many of us are focused on automation tools and technologies, it's often helpful to step back and think about how we can maximize the usefulness of our automation for our stakeholders. It can be helpful to consider the 'management' perspective of our automation work, whether or not we are officially 'managers'.

Subtopics:
* How do you make sure your test automation is focused on meeting business/stakeholder needs?
* How will you (and others) know if your automation project is 'successful' - and what does 'success' mean in your context?
* How will you (and your stakeholders) keep track of progress if there are multiple people on your automation project?
* What are things to consider in choosing an automation tool/framework?
* What considerations should go into test automation design? How is test automation design different than design of non-automation software projects?
* Will there be only 'happy path automation' or something beyond that? How can you be most effective in determining what to automate and prioritize?
